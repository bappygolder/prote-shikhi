# Design Direction — Bornomala

**Last updated**: 2026-05-04

---

## Current Design Read

The current app design is quiet, mobile-first, and teacher-tool oriented. It feels influenced by a simple classroom flashcard: one large centered Bangla letter, minimal distraction, and two clear grading actions.

That direction is right for the MVP. The learner should not see helper text, decorative UI, or too many choices while practicing recognition.

---

## What Is Working

- The Bangla glyph is the visual priority.
- The app avoids a marketing-style first screen.
- Right/Wrong actions are easy to reach.
- Session stats are visible but not dominant.
- Rounded corners are restrained and consistent.
- The palette is calm and readable.

---

## What May Be Influenced By

The UI looks closer to:

- physical flashcards
- early literacy classroom materials
- simple quiz/training apps
- paper-like study tools

It does not currently look like a heavily branded consumer app. That is fine for the first teacher/student workflow.

---

## Design Risks

| Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Beige paper tone may feel too generic later | Keep for MVP; revisit after brand rename |
| Teacher controls may distract learner if phone is facing learner | Add a teacher mode/help layer before complex visual changes |
| Stats labels may not be obvious to new users | Add an info/help overlay |
| No public-facing identity yet | Add a landing page after the app flow is useful |

---

## Direction Going Forward

Keep the practice screen sparse and functional. Add explanation through an optional info icon, not permanent instructional text. Give the future landing page more brand warmth, but keep the in-app trainer clean and practical.
